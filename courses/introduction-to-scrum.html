Introduction to Scrum

Course Summary

This one day Introduction to Scrum course covers the Scrum framework including a full explanation and open discussion about the goals and importance of the roles, meetings, and artifacts in Scrum. It aims to provide participants with enough knowledge for them to start doing Scrum in their organizations.

The course covers the five core concepts for competing on change, iterative development, the benefits of Scrum, the product backlog, and the activities inside a Sprint. Participants shall learn working in iterations and working as part of a team.

This course counts as one day towards the Certified Scrum Developer qualification. Please refer to our highly-regarded Certified Scrum Developer course for further details.

This course is endorsed by the Scrum Alliance® as part of the Scrum Alliance Registered Education Provider (REP)℠ program.

This course is offered as a public training workshop. It is also available for private and corporate set up. Contact us for more info.

Course Objective

The course is a one-day workshop which aims for the participants to:

  • Understand the five core concepts and how they enable competing on change
  • Apply iterative development and timeboxing
  • Explain the benefits of Scrum
  • Provide an overview of the Scrum framework and its roles and responsibilities
  • Apply Scrum
  • Be aware that technical practices are important, Scrum can be scaled up, and changes in the organization are likely

Target Audience

Anyone who want to learn and use Scrum for their software projects. Previous experience and knowledge in Agile Software Development and Scrum is not a prerequisite.

This course is also a required prerequisite to Odd-e's Certified Scrum Developer course. As well as optional prerequisite to Certified ScrumMaster and Certified Scrum Product Owner courses.
